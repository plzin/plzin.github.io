Newton's Method for Permutation Polynomial Inversion

July 19, 2023

This is a proof of why Newton's method works for inverting permutation polynomials, which in the post I said might require some p-adic analysis, which is not too far off and I now figured it out. The purpose of this post is just the proof, I won't be explaining the concepts here. If you want to learn the math needed, I would suggest you check out Dexter Chua's Cambridge notes on Local filds, which is where I learned this from.

The idea is just that polynomials are power series and power series are a complete local ring, which let's us use Hensel's Lemma, which (for this purpose) can be thought of as proving the convergence of Newton's method.

The polynomial p:R[[X]][Y]↦R[[X]] is F(Y)−Y. The derivative is p′=F′(Y)−1.
